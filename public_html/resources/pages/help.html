CompRes (COMPuting REScilience) is a software prototype that quantifies the resilience of a simulated Urban Transport Network (UTN).

CompRes quantifies resilience by considering it not as "avoiding deviation from the target performance function of a system under attack", but as the "avoidance of the performance collapsing limits of a system whilst under attack".

The collapsing performance limits are found by using the Monte Carlo method to calculate a resilience region.If the performance of the UTN stays withing the resilience region, the dynamical system is resilient.Otherwise, it is not.

  • It shows a mechanism (resilience region) to express resilience without finding the optimal target performance function: TP(t).
  • Calculating the collapsing performance limits of the resilience region could be easier than finding the TP(t) of the system.
  • It does not consider maximum performance as the TP(t) of the system and therefore, maximum resilience is not obtained at 100% system output.
  • It does not assume that a less resilient system should be closer to 0% performance. A system does not require to be non functioning to be useless.
  • The resilience region is easy to use and to understand. It has predictable data consumption.
  • Allows resilience to be seen as optimisation-oriented resilience, opening the door for machine learning techniques.

Ubran Transport Networks, or UTNs for short, are our dynamical system use case.

UTNs refer to the infrastructure that allows the delivery of commodities across cities.

They are a subset of Critical Infrastructure Systems (CISc) that delivers:

  • Vehicles
    • Rails, roads, distributors, streets, airports, docks, etc.
  • Water
    • Aqueducts, pipe lines, etc.
  • Power
    • Electric grids, gas pipelines, petro distribution, gasoline supply, etc.
  • Information
    • Telecomunication infrastructure, fibre optic, cellular antennas, satellites, etc.

This version of CompRes simulates a robot-car system in which they travel in a simple loop (circuite race track).

Robot-cars try to go from point A to point B at maximum possible velocity. Once they reach the reach B they are feed to A (Loop).

si represents the stakeholder that drives mi. si+1 represents the stakeholder that drives mi+1

CompRes collects input paramaters that describe a UTN and outputs graphs with results.

CompRes uses these parameters to create two comparison scenarios, typically Control vs Attack, or Attack vs Attack.

This prototype was designed, coded and implemented by David Enrique Sanchez Oliva at Newcastle University, UK, during 2022 as part of a research Towards Calculating the Resilience of a Urban Transport Network under Attack. The research is supervised by Dr. Charles Morisset.

The tool includes the datasets that were used to create this article presented in PASM'22 (11th International Workshop on Practical Applications of Stochastic Modelling). This workshop is collocated with EPEW 2022 (18th European Performance Engineering Workshop).

The datasets are just runs of the sample 1, 2, and 3. However, due to the stochastic nature of the simulator, it is unlikely that you can reproduce them exactly as they were by running the samples again.

To use these datasets. Follow the next steps:

  • Download the datasets
    • This is possible by clicking the button Datasets located next to the Samples menu.
  • Uncompress
    • This has to be done with any third-party unzip compressor.
  • Load
    • Press the Load button and point to the unzip folder.
